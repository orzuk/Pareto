Connection between /mnt/c/Code/Github/Pareto and the correlation inequality proof
==================================================================================

The Pareto repo computes exact probabilities for Pareto-optimal vectors in [0,1]^k.
The main open problem (in NewProof/) is the variance inequality:

    Var(V) <= E[V](1-E[V]) * (1 - ((m+1)/(m+2))^k)

where V = Vol(union of m random anchored boxes in [0,1]^k), m = n-2.

Key connections:
- pareto.py::pareto_E_Z1Z2_python(k,n) computes E[Z1*Z2] = P(both X_1,X_2 Pareto maxima)
  This is related to E[(1-V)^2] = P(X_1 and X_2 both non-dominated by X_3..Xn)
  via: E[Z1*Z2] = E[(1-V)^2] - P(X_2 dominates X_1 | both non-dominated) * ...
  More precisely, pareto_P_Bj1c_and_Bj2c_python gives E[(1-V)^2] directly.

- pareto.py::pareto_P_Bj1c(k,n) computes P(X_1 non-dominated) = 1 - E[V]

- The variance of the number of Pareto maxima is:
    Var(M_n) = n*p*(1-p) + n*(n-1)*(E[Z1*Z2] - p^2)
  The correlation inequality is equivalent to bounding the covariance term.

- PartialOrders.py studies a generalization (DNF/CNF ordering inequalities)
  The "matrix inequality" special case IS the correlation inequality.
